Our History

A Spark of Innovation (2044)

The Nuka-Cola Corporation was established in 2044 by visionary entrepreneur John-Caleb Bradberton. Fueled by a desire to redefine the soft drink industry, Bradberton set out to create a beverage that would become a symbol of American culture and innovation. His original formula, featuring a unique 120% daily recommended dose of sugar and 17 different fruit essences, launched Nuka-Cola into immediate nationwide popularity.

Becoming a National Icon (2045–2050)

Within a year of launch, Nuka-Cola was being bottled and distributed across the entire continental United States. Strategic partnerships with local bottling plants and aggressive advertising campaigns—often featuring the now-iconic Nuka-Girl—cemented the brand’s presence in American households. The company expanded its product line to include several popular variants, including Nuka-Cherry, Nuka-Cola Dark, and the highly-caffeinated Nuka-Cola Wild.

Nuka-World: More Than a Theme Park (2050)

In 2050, the company unveiled its most ambitious endeavor yet: Nuka-World, a state-of-the-art amusement park dedicated to the world of Nuka-Cola. Located just outside of Boston, Nuka-World served not only as a family entertainment destination, but also as a showcase of the company’s values: joy, innovation, and Americana. The park featured several themed zones, live entertainment, product research facilities, and cutting-edge beverage development labs.

Expanding Horizons Through Research

Nuka-Cola's dedication to progress led to the establishment of the Beverage Innovation Division, where researchers—often referred to internally as "Beverageers"—worked tirelessly to refine existing formulas and explore new frontiers. This commitment to innovation culminated in the release of Nuka-Cola Quantum in 2076, a groundbreaking drink featuring a unique isotope-powered luminescence and bold citrus taste.

Meet Our Founder

Leadership & Legacy

Founder John-Caleb Bradberton remained at the helm of the corporation until late 2077, overseeing its transformation into one of the most influential companies in the United States. Under his leadership, Nuka-Cola became more than a beverage—it became a cultural institution. Though the world has changed, the Nuka-Cola name remains a lasting symbol of American ingenuity, optimism, and refreshment.

A Message from Our Founder

"When I created Nuka-Cola, I wasn’t just bottling a beverage—I was bottling the spirit of a nation. In every fizzy drop, I saw the future: bright, bold, and unapologetically American. I believed then, as I do now, that refreshment should be more than just a moment—it should be an experience. One that brings families together, powers long road trips, and offers a sip of joy in even the most uncertain times. From the first bottle off the line to the launch of Nuka-World, my vision has remained the same: to bring people something they can believe in. That’s the Nuka-Cola promise. And that promise will stand the test of time."
John-Caleb Bradberton - Founder & Visionary, Nuka-Cola Corporation
